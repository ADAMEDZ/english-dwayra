Full session plans for Cancel Culture and Ambition debates

Cancel culture: justice or mob mentality?
Debate 90 min Week 14 · Monday Hot topic — ground rules required
Before the session — manager prep
Assign sides randomly 24h before — don't let members choose their own side.
Write 3 real examples on the board before they arrive: J.K. Rowling, Johnny Depp, local social media pile-ons.
Set one ground rule out loud at the start: "We debate the idea, not the person. No personal attacks."
Session timeline
0:00
10 min
Warm-up — quick takes

Each member answers in 1 sentence: "Name one person or brand that was cancelled. Was it fair?" No debate yet — just get talking.

0:10
5 min
Define the motion

Manager reads aloud: "Cancel culture is mob mentality dressed as justice." Sides revealed. 5 min to huddle and list 3 arguments.

0:15
6 min
Opening statements

Each side gets 3 min. No interruptions. Moderator times strictly.

0:21
20 min
Open debate — round 1

Free-flowing, moderator controls the floor. Push shy members: "You haven't spoken — what do you think?" Keep it on track.

0:41
4 min
Rebuttal prep

Each side has 2 min to write their strongest counter to what they just heard.

0:45
10 min
Rebuttal round

Each side delivers rebuttal (2 min each). Then open floor for 6 min of cross-questioning.

0:55
10 min
Open floor — undecided members

Anyone not on a side now speaks. What convinced them? What didn't? This is the richest part.

1:05
6 min
Closing statements

Each side gets 3 min. Must reference something the other side said.

1:11
9 min
Debrief + vote

Group votes: who argued better (not who they agree with). Manager gives language feedback — top 3 expressions used well, top 3 mistakes heard.

1:20
—
Close

Announce next session topic. Give prep prompt if needed.

Arguments bank — both sides
Side A — It's justice
Powerful people historically faced no consequences — cancellation fills a legal gap
Social pressure is a legitimate democratic tool, not violence
Victims of abuse deserve public validation, not silence
Companies and celebrities profit from public trust — losing it is a fair consequence
It's not permanent — many "cancelled" people return within months
Side B — It's mob mentality
Punishment without due process or evidence — trial by Twitter
Context is always stripped — one old tweet destroys a career
People pile on for social approval, not genuine moral concern
It silences people and kills honest public discourse
Disproportionate — minor mistakes get the same treatment as serious crimes
Manager's intervention questions — use when debate slows
What's the difference between cancellation and accountability?
If someone apologizes, should they be forgiven? Who decides?
Would you cancel a close friend for the same thing you'd cancel a celebrity?
Is silence on social media the same as supporting someone?

Is ambition a blessing or a curse?
Discussion 60 min Week 13 · Monday Open discussion — no assigned sides
Before the session — manager prep
Write this quote on the board before they arrive: "Ambition is the last refuge of the failure." — Oscar Wilde. Don't explain it. Let them react.
Have 2 contrasting examples ready: Elon Musk (built rockets, destroyed his own company's culture) and a local overworked student who burned out.
Session timeline
0:00
5 min
Warm-up — rate yourself

Each member rates their own ambition 1–10 out of loud. No explanation yet — just numbers. Creates instant curiosity and comparison.

0:05
10 min
Define it first

Group answers: what is ambition exactly? Is it the same as greed? Same as motivation? Manager writes key words on board as they speak.

0:15
15 min
Round 1 — personal stories

Each member shares a moment where their ambition helped them OR hurt them. 1–2 min each. No commenting yet — just listening.

0:30
15 min
Round 2 — open debate

Now challenge each other's stories. Manager picks the most interesting story from round 1 and opens the floor: "Does everyone agree this was ambition, or something else?"

0:45
8 min
Big question vote

Manager asks: "Ambitious people — do they make the world better or worse?" Everyone votes yes/no then defends in 30 seconds.

0:53
7 min
Debrief + language feedback

Manager highlights best expressions used, common grammar mistakes heard, and gives the group one phrase to practice before next session.

Discussion angles — push these if the group goes shallow
Ambition as blessing
Drives invention — without ambition there's no progress
Gives life direction and meaning beyond survival
Ambitious people raise the bar for everyone around them
The alternative — complacency — is arguably worse
Ambition as curse
Destroys relationships — always chasing the next thing
Never satisfied — success never feels like enough
Creates ruthlessness — stepping over others to climb
Culturally imposed — who decided ambition is a virtue?
Manager's intervention questions
Can someone be too ambitious? Where's the line?
Is ambition different for men and women in our culture?
Would you rather be ambitious and unhappy, or content and average?
Does ambition require sacrificing something — and is that sacrifice always worth it?
Roles for this session
Moderator
You (the manager). Keep time, pull in quiet members, redirect when off-topic.
Devil's advocate
Assign one member to always argue the opposite of whatever the group agrees on.
Note-taker
One member writes the best quotes and arguments on the board in real time.
